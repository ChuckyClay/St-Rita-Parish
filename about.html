About the Parish

Parish History

St. Rita Parish was established to serve the spiritual needs of the Nkomo Ward community, bordering Kagaene, Kianjai, and Ruiri parishes. The parish is comprised of seven prayer houses, each fostering faith, unity, and service. Over the years, St. Rita Parish has grown into a vibrant center for worship, education, and outreach, supporting families and individuals in their spiritual journeys. The parish is committed to upholding Catholic traditions while embracing the unique culture and needs of its members.


Patron Saint: Saint Rita of Cascia

Saint Rita of Cascia (c. 1381 - 1457) is one of the most beloved saints in the Catholic Church. Known as the Saint of the Impossible and the Patroness of hopeless cases, she is a powerful intercessor, especially in family and marital struggles. Born in Italy, Saint Rita endured many hardships with faith and patience, including a difficult marriage and the loss of her children. She later joined the Augustinian nuns and became renowned for her charity, humility, and devotion. Her feast day is celebrated on May 22nd, and she continues to inspire countless faithful around the world.


Mission

To proclaim the Gospel of Jesus Christ, celebrate the sacraments, and serve our community with love, compassion, and integrity. We strive to foster spiritual growth, encourage active participation, and support all members in their faith journey.

Vision

To be a welcoming parish where all are invited to encounter Christ, grow in faith, and participate fully in the life and mission of the Church. We envision a community united in prayer, service, and fellowship, making a positive impact in the world.
